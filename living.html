Trash, recycling & big stuff

  • Three carts — trash, recycling, and organics (yard + food waste). Out by 6 a.m. on your collection day, back in that evening.
  • Your collection day — one day covers the whole community. Look it up on Burrtec ↗ (we'll post the exact day here once it's confirmed).
  • Free bulky-item pickup — up to 4 times a year, 3 large items each (furniture, appliances, branches). Schedule at least 48 hours ahead via Green Santa Clarita ↗.
  • Paint, batteries, electronics, propane — never in the carts. Drop-off at Burrtec, 26000 Springbrook Ave (Mon–Fri, plus the 3rd Saturday morning).
  • Illegal dumping in the common areas — report to the City Stormwater Hotline, (661) 222-7222.

Living in a fire zone — staying insured

  • We're in high fire-hazard country. CAL FIRE maps much of the Santa Clarita Valley — including around Tesoro Highlands — as high or very-high fire hazard. Check your parcel: CAL FIRE hazard-zone map ↗.
  • It's why insurers are getting strict. Non-renewals and steep premiums are common here. The single biggest lever you control is mitigation.
  • Harden your home & clear defensible space — California's standard is up to 100 feet (Public Resources Code 4291). This is exactly what the common-area brush by the water towers is about. Start with CAL FIRE's Ready for Wildfire ↗.
  • Document it for a discount. The state's "Safer from Wildfires" program (CA Dept. of Insurance) ↗ requires insurers to credit specific mitigation steps — and can move a home from "uninsurable" back to insurable.
  • Can't find coverage? The California FAIR Plan ↗ is the state's insurer of last resort — (800) 339-4099.
💡 A community win worth chasing: if Tesoro Highlands earns Firewise USA ↗ recognition, many insurers apply a 5–15% premium discount to every home in the neighborhood — and it builds directly on the brush-clearance work. One for the HOA agenda.
General resources, not insurance advice — talk to a licensed agent about your own policy.
