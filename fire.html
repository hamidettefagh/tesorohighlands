🫁 When it's smoky — the playbooktap to open
Shared by a neighbor in the community group chat, lightly edited.
  • Seal the house. Close all windows and doors.
  • AC on recirculate. Set it to "Circulate," and switch the fan from Auto to On so it keeps filtering. Skip whole-house fans — they pull smoke in.
  • Run air purifiers. Box fans help push the clean air around.
  • Don't make indoor smoke. No candles, fireplace, frying or broiling, and no vacuuming until the air clears.
  • Outside, wear a real N95 or N100 — snug to the face. Cloth and surgical masks don't stop smoke. N95s don't fit babies or toddlers, so keep little ones in your cleanest room.
  • Drink plenty of water. Smoke dries out eyes, throat, and sinuses.
  • Watch the vulnerable — kids, older neighbors, and anyone with asthma or heart or lung issues.
  • If it gets thick and hot, leave for cleaner air instead of toughing it out.
