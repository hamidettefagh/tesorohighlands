🫁 When it's smoky — the playbooktap to open
Shared by a neighbor in the community group chat, lightly edited.
  • Seal the house. Close all windows and doors.
  • AC on recirculate. Set it to "Circulate," and switch the fan from Auto to On so it keeps filtering. Skip whole-house fans — they pull smoke in.
  • Run air purifiers. Box fans help push the clean air around.
  • Don't make indoor smoke. No candles, fireplace, frying or broiling, and no vacuuming until the air clears.
  • Outside, wear a real N95 or N100 — snug to the face. Cloth and surgical masks don't stop smoke. N95s don't fit babies or toddlers, so keep little ones in your cleanest room.
  • Drink plenty of water. Smoke dries out eyes, throat, and sinuses.
  • Watch the vulnerable — kids, older neighbors, and anyone with asthma or heart or lung issues.
  • If it gets thick and hot, leave for cleaner air instead of toughing it out.

Your community

🚁
Four shared water tanks in the community. Helicopters may refill here during a fire — that low, loud pass overhead is them working, not a reason to panic.
⚠️
Known brush hazard. Dried brush sits in the common areas by the water towers and up Avenida Rancho Tesoro. It's the closest fuel to our homes — report it and push to clear it before peak season.
📖
Why it matters. Last year the Hughes Fire started 6–7 mi away and its evacuation zones reached within 3–4 mi as it blew past 10,000 acres in a single day. Two fires in two years have come within about 4 miles.

Why this matters for the brush by the water towers: fire reaching this valley is a return interval, not a possibility. California's defensible-space standard (Public Resources Code 4291) calls for up to 100 feet of managed space around homes — clearing the common-area brush is exactly what that standard exists for.
